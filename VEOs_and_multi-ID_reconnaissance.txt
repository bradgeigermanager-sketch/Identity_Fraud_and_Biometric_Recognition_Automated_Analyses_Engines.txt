# Multi‑ID Reconnaissance: What It Is  
**Multi‑ID reconnaissance** is a hostile intelligence behavior where a single operative uses **multiple identities** — legal, forged, synthetic, or stolen — to:

- Test security boundaries  
- Probe identity‑verification systems  
- Map which identity attributes trigger scrutiny  
- Identify which compartments contain sensitive data  
- Attempt to locate or confirm the existence of a **protected official’s hidden identity**

This is not “fraud” in the conventional sense.  
It is **hostile reconnaissance disguised as fraud**.

# How Multi‑ID Reconnaissance Works (High‑Level Security Analysis)

## 1. **Identity Probing**
A single operative cycles through multiple identities:

- Different names  
- Different nationalities  
- Different documents  
- Different biometric enrollment attempts  

Each identity is used to interact with:

- Border systems  
- Government portals  
- Public‑facing services  
- Infrastructure access points  

The operative watches **how the system reacts**.

---

## 2. **Biometric Collision Testing**
The operative deliberately reuses the **same face** across multiple identities.

Your system detects this as:

- **Multi‑ID / Single‑Face anomaly**  
- A high‑confidence biometric collision  
- A potential reconnaissance pattern

This is where your CI pipeline activates.

---

## 3. **Protected‑Identity Mapping**
If the operative’s multi‑ID attempts occur:

- Near the protected official’s hidden identity  
- Near the official’s movement patterns  
- Near the official’s secret compartments  
- Near the time window of a failed attack  

…then the multi‑ID anomaly is **not random fraud**.  
It is **targeted reconnaissance**.

This is the moment your CI DAG treats the anomaly as a **hostile intelligence signal**.

---

## 4. **Compartment Pressure Testing**
The operative tries to see:

- Which identity gets flagged  
- Which identity gets through  
- Which identity triggers deeper checks  
- Which identity triggers no checks  

This reveals **which compartments contain sensitive data**.

Your CI pipeline uses this behavior to infer:

- Which compartment leaked  
- Which internal actor or system is compromised  
- Which identity attributes the hostile actor is trying to reach

---

## 5. **Reconnaissance → Attack Pipeline**
In VEO behavior (again: responsible for severe harm and human‑rights violations), multi‑ID reconnaissance is often a precursor to:

- Target selection  
- Route planning  
- Identity infiltration  
- Attempted access to protected individuals  
- Pre‑attack staging

Your CI pipeline is designed to detect this **before** the attack stage.
